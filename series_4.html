introduction

Talking about Invalidators of fasting in Islaam, we are talking about those acts that will render our fast in annulment and inutile, if done. It is very important to know all these nullifiers of ·π£owm (fasting) which shall be discussed subsequently.

what are the nullifiers of ·π£owm?

The question as to what are the nullifiers or invalidators of ·π¢owm could best be answered under two headings:

  1. Invalidators which require paying back only, and
  2. Invalidators which require paying back and making of specified atonement.

the discussion

1. Invalidators which require paying back only (termed as Al-Qodoo')

This comprises those nullifiers upon which a fasting person is required and mandated to make up the fast at later date. This requires the victim, whose Romadon fast becomes annulled, to have the annulled fast paid back after which the month of Romadon has elapsed. Nullifiers and Invalidators under this heading includes the following:

  • Intentional passage of food and/or drink into the stomach, and not out of nisyaan (forgetfulness). That is, intentional eating at Romadon day break by a fasting person.
  • Vomiting intentionally.
  • Coming out of menstrual flow or that of post-childbirth, even it happens a minute before the sun set.
  • Masturbation and coming out of al-maniyy (sperm), intentionally and without forgetfulness.
  • Having the intention of breaking the fast when not at right time of breaking the fast.
  • Denouncing the religion of Islaam before the time of if-toor (fast breaking). And provided he, at later time, refuted his initial declaration and thus, returned back to the religion of Islaam, he has to make up that particular fast because his earlier declaration has rendered the fast annulled.

NOTE: If any of these, by any circumstance, happened, the affected days are to be made-up after the end of Romadon.

2. Invalidators which require paying back and making of atonement (Al-qodoo' wal-kaffaaroh)

Under this, there is only one nullifier. And that is 'Having sexual relation' during Romadon fast. The hadiith provided below is so well instructive. Read:

"Narrated Abu Huraira: While we were sitting with the Prophet (Ô∑∫) a man came and said, "O Allah's Messenger (Ô∑∫)! I have been ruined." Allah's Messenger (Ô∑∫) asked what was the matter with him. He replied "I had sexual intercourse with my wife while I was fasting." Allah's Messenger (Ô∑∫) asked him, "Can you afford to manumit (free) a slave?" He replied in the negative. Allah's Messenger (Ô∑∫) asked him, "Can you fast for two successive months?" He replied in the negative. The Prophet (Ô∑∫) asked him, "Can you afford to feed sixty poor persons?" He replied in the negative. The Prophet (Ô∑∫) kept silent and while we were in that state, a big basket full of dates was brought to the Prophet (Ô∑∫) . He asked, "Where is the questioner?" He replied, "I (am here)." The Prophet (Ô∑∫) said (to him), "Take this (basket of dates) and give it in charity." The man said, "Should I give it to a person poorer than I? By Allah; there is no family between its (i.e. Medina's) two mountains who are poorer than I." The Prophet (Ô∑∫) smiled till his premolar teeth became visible and then said, 'Feed your family with it." (Reported by Bukoriy).

Behold! It is still subject of contention among Islamic jurists, as to whether a victim of this is required to pay back the affected day in addition to the stipulated atonement as clearly stated in the prophetic saying above.

However, the outweighing opinion is that only the atonement is required. Allaah knows best.
