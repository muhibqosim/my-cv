non-invalidators of Ramadan Fast

üôé M.Q Atanda     ‚è±Ô∏è April 10, 2021

introduction

Successfully, the nullifiers cum invalidators of ·π£owm (fasting) have been succintly discussed in the previous discussion, precisely series 4. Therefore, this discourse shall center on the non-nullifiers of fast in islaam. Put differently, those things or acts which, if does, renders not the fast a nullity.

The non-nullifiers:

1. Bathing during the day: Bathing does not invalidate the fast.

2. Tasting the food: Tasting food, if there is strong reason to do that, does not invalidate the fast unless the substance passes through the throat.

3. Rinsing the mouth: It is permissible and not of the invalidators. However, it must be done with caution and lightly. The water must not pass through the throat.

4. Cupping and blood transfusion: The consensus opinion is that cupping and blood transfusion are both not invalidators of ·π£owm (fast). Nevertheless, it was held that:

  • it is disliked if the person undergoing cupping and the person undergoing blood transfusion would be affected. And also that,
  • it is not accepted to undergo any of it provided the person would be prompted to have the fast break. That is, it is at best if it could be done after the fast breaking time. Allaah knows best.

5. Applying eye drop: If for health purposes, one is compelled to apply eye drop, there is no problem with that.

6. Taking Injection: Taking Injection that is not nutritive during the fasting period does not invalidate the fast. However, for those having their nutrition through injection, as contained in the book sohiih fiq-hus-sunnah, whether or not such injection contains nutritive substances, it does not nullify their fast.

7. Brushing the teeth: This act does not also invalidate the fast. Notwithstanding, the Shaafi`iyyah (Shafis school of thought) and the Hambaliyyah (Hambalis school of thought) are still of the opinion of suspending the use of brush till after iftoor (fast breaking time). However, they did not say it invalidates the fast.

8. The extraction of mucus: Mucus coming out of the nostrils does not render the fast in annulment.

9. Unintentional eating, drinking and vomiting: Based on the Hadiith reported from the prophet, eating or drinking unconsciously does not invalidates the fast. However, if one persist and continue eating, even if it is just a mouthful, after he has recollected that he is fasting, then, his fast is no more intact and therefore has to pay back that day. In the same vein, vomiting, unintentionally, has no effect on the fast.

10. Being in state of junub (i.e impurity): Impurity, in this context, is that liquid discharge that comes out from either of the two genders' organs be it through copulation or as a result of "casting a look" on one's spouse or having a wet dream. This is differentiated from masturbation.

Relying on the hadiith reported by Imaam Bukoriyy that says; if someone get discharged after casting a look on his wife, his fast is still intact .

Also, that: He who gets up in the morning being in state of janabah after having affairs with his wife the previous night should just take his ritual bath and continue his fast .

And as for he who had a wet dream, unless the discharge is through masturbation or that he himself is the cause of the discharge (through prohibited means like watching a pornography) , such does not nullify his fast. So also, the females.

These are some of the non-invalidators of ·π£owm (fasting). Only Him knows best.
