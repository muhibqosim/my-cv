SERIES 2
Romadoon Fast: Ruling, Virtues and its Terms and Conditions.

introduction:

Having established, in the first series, what constitutes fasting in Islaam, the various classifications and types of fasting, and the importance of fasting, we therefore proceed to discussing, shortly, concisely and precisely, the rulings, virtues, terms and components of Romadoon fast.

the ruling:

Ṣowmu Romadoon (fasting in the blessed month of Romadoon) is, undebatable, one of the pillars of the religion of islaam and even the fourth after zakaat (charity).

Ṣowmu Romadoon is one which its observance is made compulsory on every able Muslim: who has reached the stage of puberty, free from any form of insanity and health challenges, and that he is muqiim (dweller of a given geographical location and not a traveller).

Two selected from among the numerous evidences stating its compulsoriness are given below:

• First is the evidence from the Qur-aan, Q2 vs 183 (Al-Baqoroh):

"O you who have believed, decreed upon you is fasting as it was decreed upon those before you that you may become righteous".

• The other one is a saying of the prophet of islaam (may the peace of Allaah be upon him) as it was reported by Bukhoriy:

"Islaam is you worshipping Allaah and not associating partner with him, observing solaat, paying the compulsory zakaah (charity), and fasting in the month of Romadoon...".

the virtues:

Talking about the virtues of Romadoon. It should be understood that the month of Romadoon, the ninth month, is the best of all months. Below are some of the virtues of the month:

  1. All acts of righteousness are rewarded in manifolds.
  2. During this period, the doors of hell are closed and while the gates leading to paradise are widely opened.
  3. The devil and his cohorts are as well chained.
  4. For he who fasts during this period, and with the undiluted intent of attaining piety and sincerity, his previous sin, except for kabaair (the major sins), will all be forgiven.
  5. Also, in its last ten days lies the "best night" - a night better-off a thousand of months in virtues.

terms and conditions:

The terms and conditions applying to the validity of Romadoon fast are of two things:

A. Purification from impurities:

This is peculiar to the women of Islamic faith. It connotes purification from State of Hayd (mentrual circle) and Nifaas (blood that flows during the postpartum period).

B. intention:

Fasting is said to be an act of worship; it must be accompanied with a sincere and specified intention (niyyah). For this condition to be met, each an individual intending to fast must specifically either on daily basis or on the first night of Romadoon make up his "intent" to fast.

NOTE: As regards to the intention, it is to be made in the mind only and not in words.

components of romadoon fast:

This refers to the do's and don'ts of fasting for it to be acceptable. It could also be discussed under two headings, viz:

A. Pillarsof fasting:

This includes the abstinence from all that (like eating, drinking, intercourse, being unconscious, and so on) which could invalidate one's fast from the dawn till the sunset. This is much explained in Q2 vs 187.

B. recommended acts:

As opposed to the first components which, if done, invalidates the fast, this includes: taking the pre-dawn meal (sahuur) and taking it at the last hour; hastening in breaking the fast (at its due time); praying before having the fast breaking; charity, recitation of Qur-aan and teaching it; guarding the tongue from irritative and unrighteous talk, and parting away from the peace-disturber. .

However, if any of this is omitted, the fast is still intact.

SERIES 1
The concept of fasting: meaning, classification and importance.

introduction:

This writing explains, although very brief but concise, what ṣowm (fasting) means in the religion of islaam, the various classifications of ṣowm in islaamic religion and essence of fasting.

meaning:

Ṣowm, otherwise called Siyaam, is an Arabic word which linguistically means: alim-tisaak (abstinence) and al-kaff (discontinuance).

And while, in technical sense (according to the understanding of the sharii-`ah), ṣowm or ṣiyaam is that act of abstaining and discontinuance with all that, which can invalidate our fast starting from the appearance of al-fajr (dawn, before day break) till the setting of the sun (ghuruubish-sham-s) and which is accompanied with the intent of worshipping Allaah, the Most High.

classifications:

According to the author of Sohiihu fiq-hus-sunnah (rohimahul-laah), there are basically two (2) classifications of sowm:

1. al-waajib (obligatory)

By waajib, in Islamic jurisprudence, we mean those acts which are compulsory on each an individual and which is punishable upon neglect. So therefore, Ṣawmun waajib (compulsory fast or fasting) is that kind of sowm which is compulsory for each an individual to observe, and which upon neglect or disregard, the omitter is punishable. Simply put, it comprises those kinds of fasts which each and every individual Muslims is under obligation to observe.

This is further classified into some sub-classications:

  • That which is obligatory for a specific period of time: this include only Ṣowm Romadoon (Ramadan fast) which lasts for either 29 or 30 days throughout the month of Romadoon.
  • That which is obligatory for a cause or flaw: this is quite known as Ṣowmul-Kaffaaroh (atonement or expiation fast).
  • That which is obligatory by way of making a vow: that is, a self-obligatory. This is called Ṣowmun-Nadh-r.

2. at-tatow-wu`u (voluntary)

This means, as opposed to al-waajib, non-obligatory. And each an individual is under discretion to fast or not. This further includes:

  • Sittatu Shaw-waal: the six fast in the month of Shaw-waal after the completion of Romadoon.
  • Taasuu-`aa wa `aashuuroo: the fast in the ninth and tenth month of Shaw-waal.
  • Ṣowmu shah-ru Sha`baan: fasting in the month of Sha`baan.
  • Ṣowmu yawmul-`arofah: fasting on the day of `Arofah (a day preceding the tenth day of Dhul-hijjah
  • Ṣowmul-Khomiis walith-nayn: fasting on the every Monday and Thursday of the week
  • Ṣowmu ayyaamul-biid: fasting on 13th, 14th or 15th day of every Islamic month.
  • Ṣowmu ayyaamul-biid: fasting on 13th, 14th or 15th day of every Islamic month.

importance:

The importance of fasting cannot be overemphasized. Thus, below are few:

1. Fasting is form of Obedience to Allaah, and by it:

  • Man moves closer to his Lord.
  • a devoter is rewarded without a set limitation.
  • forgiveness of previous sins is guaranteed
  • protection against hell fire.
  • assurance of Jannah (paradise) through the special made door for those who fast

2. Fasting is like a Special Moral School to the believers as it instill in them:

  • attainment of perfect moral character.
  • Self-jihad (self-sacrifice).
  • leniency to one another.
  • Perseverance
  • strengthening of Muslim brotherhood.

Purification in Islaam (At-tohaaroh)

Tohaaroh, to simply put, means "purity or purification". It is considered as waajib, a must. This is clearly stated in Q 74 vs 4 and an hadith that: "Purity is half of faith".

In islaam, waajib is what is regarded as compulsory or mandatory. And as such, its omission attracts punishment.

Categorically, it is categorised into two: Zoohirah and Baatinah (outerpart and innerpart purification).

The baatinah is the purity of nafs (the heart, mind) from sins and dis-obedience. And it is done by wat is called "REPENTANCE". Also, purifying the soul from shirk (associating partner with Allah), enviness and all other which are regarded as impure acts.

Allaah said in Q 91 vs 9 & 10 (Ash-shams):
"He has succeeded who purifies it."
"And he has failed who instills it [with corruption]."

Zoohiroh, outerpart purification, on the other side, is also divided into two sub-branches: al-khubth and al-hadath .

The khabath which is regarded as purity from offensive condition is associated with the removal of impurity from cloth, human body and place of prayer with "CLEAN WATER" only or any of its substitutes.

And the second sub-branch which is AL-HADATH involves either of wuduu (water ablution), ghusl (spiritual bath) and tayammun (dry or sand ablution). It is called ritual purification. Q 5 vs 6 (Al-maaidah) is instructive here. It says: "O you who have believed, when you rise to [perform] prayer, wash your faces and your forearms to the elbows and wipe over your heads and wash your feet to the ankles. And if you are in a state of janabah, then purify yourselves. But if you are ill or on a journey or one of you comes from the place of relieving himself or you have contacted women and do not find water, then seek clean earth and wipe over your faces and hands with it..."
