introduction

Having established, in the first series, what constitutes fasting in Islaam, the various categories of fasting and, its importance, we, therefore, proceed to series two of the Pre-Romadon series. The topic as couched above is tilted towards discussing, very concise and precise, the rulings, virtues, terms and components of Romadoon fast. So, we proceed!

the ruling

·π¢owmu Romadon (fasting in the blessed month of Romadon) is, undebatable, one of the pillars of the religion of islaam. It takes the fourth position after zakaat (charity).

·π¢owmu Romadon is one which its observance is made compulsory on every able Muslim who has reached the stage of puberty, free from any form of insanity and health challenges, and that he is muqiim (dweller of a given geographical location and not a traveller).

Two selected from among the numerous evidences stating its compulsoriness are given below:

‚Ä¢ First is the evidence from the Qur-aan, Q2 vs 183 (Al-Baqoroh):

"O you who have believed, decreed upon you is fasting as it was decreed upon those before you that you may become righteous".

‚Ä¢ The other one is a saying of the prophet of islaam (may the peace of Allaah be upon him) as it was reported by Bukhoriy:

"Islaam is you worshipping Allaah and not associating partner with him, observing solaat, paying the compulsory zakaah (charity), and fasting in the month of Romadon...".

the virtues

Talking about the virtues of Romadoon. It should be understood that the month of Romadon, the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar, is the best of all months. Below are some of the virtues of the month:

  1. All acts of righteousness are rewarded in manifolds.
  2. During this period, the doors of hell are closed and while the gates leading to paradise are widely opened.
  3. For he who fasts during this period, and with the undiluted intent of attaining piety and sincerity, his previous sin, except for kabaair (the major sins), will all be forgiven. That is, previously committed minor sins are forgiven only for fasting in this blessed month.
  4. The devil and his cohorts are as well chained.
  5. Also, in its last ten days lies the "best night" - a night better-off a thousand of months in virtues. The nigt is known as laylatul-qodr.

terms and conditions

The terms and conditions applying to the validity of Romadon fast are of two things:

A. Purification from impurities

This is peculiar to the women of Islamic faith. It connotes purification from state of Hayd (mentrual circle) and Nifaas (blood that flows during the postpartum period).

B. intention

Fasting is said to be an act of worship and so, it must be accompanied with a sincere and specified intention (niyyah). For this condition to be met, each an individual intending to fast must specifically either on daily basis or on the first night of Romadon make up his "intent" to fast in this month.

NOTE: Regarding the intention, it is to be made only in the mind only and not in words. Hemce, no need of uttering some non-prophetic incantations (adh-kaar). You only need to have jt in mind that you would be fasting.

components of romadoon fast

This refers to the do's and don'ts of fasting for it to be acceptable. It could also be discussed under two headings, viz:

A. Pillarsof fasting

This includes the abstinence from all that (like eating, drinking, intercourse, being unconscious, and so on) which could invalidate one's fast from the dawn till the sunset. This is much explained in Q2 vs 187.

B. recommended acts

As opposed to the first components which, if done, invalidates the fast, this includes: taking the pre-dawn meal (sahuur) and taking it at the last hour; hastening in breaking the fast (at its due time); praying before having the fast breaking; charity, recitation of Qur-aan and teaching it; guarding the tongue from irritative and unrighteous talk, and parting away from the peace-disturber. .

However, if any of this is omitted, the fast is still intact.
