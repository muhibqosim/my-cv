The concept of fasting: meaning, classification and importance.

üôé M.Q Atanda     ‚è±Ô∏è April 6, 2021

introduction

This writing explains, although, very brief but concise, what ·π£owm (fasting) means in the religion of islaam, the various classifications of ·π£owm in islaamic religion cum the essence of fasting.

meaning

·π¢owm, otherwise called Siyaam, is an Arabic word which linguistically means: alim-tisaak (abstinence) and al-kaff (discontinuance).

And while, in its technical sense (according to the understanding of the Islamic sharii-`ah), ·π£owm or ·π£iyaam is that act of abstaining and discontinuance with all that, which can invalidate our fast starting from the appearance of al-fajr (dawn, before day break) till the setting of the sun (ghuruubish-sham-s) and which is accompanied with the intent of worshipping Allaah, the most High.

classifications

According to the author of Sohiihu fiq-hus-sunnah (rohimahul-laah), there are basically two (2) classifications of sowm - al-waajib (obligatory fasting) and at-tatow-wu`u (voluntary fasting). These are further explained below:

1. al-waajib (obligatory)

By al-waajib, in Islamic jurisprudence, we mean those acts which are compulsory on each an individual and which is punishable if neglected. So therefore, ·π¢awmun waajib (compulsory fast or fasting) is that kind of sowm which is compulsory for each an individual to observe, and which upon omission or disregard is punishable. Simply put, it comprises those kinds of fasts which each and every individual Muslims is under obligation to observe as their duty to Allaah.

This is further classified into some sub-classications:

  • That which is obligatory for a specific period of time: this include only ·π¢owm Romadoon (Ramadan fast) which lasts for either 29 or 30 days throughout the month of Romadoon.
  • That which is obligatory for a cause or flaw: this is quite known as ·π¢owmul-Kaffaaroh (atonement or expiation fast).
  • That which is obligatory by way of making a vow: that is, a self-obligatory. This is called, in Arabic, ·π¢owmun-Nadh-r.

2. at-tatow-wu`u (voluntary)

This means, as opposed to al-waajib, non-obligatory Fasting. And each an individual is under discretion to fast or not. This further includes:

  • Sittatu Shaw-waal: the six fast in the month of Shaw-waal after the completion of Romadoon.
  • Taasuu-`aa wa `aashuuroo: the fast in the ninth and tenth month of Shaw-waal.
  • ·π¢owmu shah-ru Sha`baan: fasting in the month of Sha`baan.
  • ·π¢owmu yawmul-`arofah: fasting on the day of `Arofah (a day preceding the tenth day of Dhul-hijjah
  • ·π¢owmul-Khomiis walith-nayn: fasting on the every Monday and Thursday of the week
  • ·π¢owmu ayyaamul-biid: fasting on 13th, 14th or 15th day of every Islamic month.
  • ·π¢owmu ayyaamul-biid: fasting on 13th, 14th and 15th day of every Islamic month.

importance

The importance of fasting cannot be overemphasized. Thus, below are few:

1. Fasting is form of Obedience to Allaah, and by it:

  • Man moves closer to his Lord.
  • a devoter is rewarded without a set limitation.
  • forgiveness of previous sins is guaranteed
  • protection against hell fire.
  • assurance of Jannah (paradise) through the special made door for those who fast

2. Fasting is like a Special Moral School to the believers as it instill in them:

  • attainment of perfect moral character.
  • Self-jihad (self-sacrifice).
  • leniency to one another.
  • Perseverance
  • strengthening of Muslim brotherhood.
