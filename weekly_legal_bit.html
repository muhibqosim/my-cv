The practice of ordering the re-adoption of Final Written Addresses by a trial Court in order to enlarge the constitutional time bound within which the court has to deliver its judgement is unknown to the law. As such, the practice cannot renew or enlarge the constitutional time bound where it elapses. In effect, it may serve as a ground for setting aside or nullifying the decision so reached by the Court.

See:
ONWUAKPA V. ONYEAMA (2022) 17 NWLR (Pt. 1858) 97 (S.C.)
